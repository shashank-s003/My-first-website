RATE EQUATION

In chemistry the rate equation(also known as the rate law or the empirical differential rate equation) is an empirical
differential mathematical expression for the reaction rate of the given reaction in trms ofconcentration of chemical
species and constant parameters (normally rate coefficient and partial orders of reaction) only. For many reactions, the
initial rate is given by a power law such as
v 0 = k[A] x [y] y
where [A] and [B] are the molar concentrations of the species A and B, usually in moles per litre (Molarity M).

Example of first order chemical reaction
2H 2O 2 ----> 2H 2O + O 2
